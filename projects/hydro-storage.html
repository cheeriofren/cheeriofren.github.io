Modified Pumped Hydro Energy Storage for Indonesia's Special Needs

Jul 2024 - Aug 2024 Associated with Columbia University

Indonesia faces significant energy challenges due to its diverse geography and varying energy demands. The country heavily relies on fossil fuels, creating a need for alternative solutions that ensure energy security while minimizing environmental impacts. Integrating geospatial technologies and data-driven analysis is pivotal in developing innovative energy solutions.

Interactive PHES System Model

Project Implementation

The project utilizes GIS and remote sensing to pinpoint optimal locations for PHES installations. By analyzing topography, hydrology, and infrastructure, it ensures effective utilization of local water resources while considering environmental impacts. The system's design incorporates variable speed pump-turbines that adapt in real time to fluctuations in demand, enhancing efficiency.

Technical Innovation

To further enhance this solution, advanced engineering techniques are tailored to Indonesia's ecological conditions. These include dynamic simulation models for performance evaluation, hydraulic optimization for energy conversion, and adaptive control systems for real-time adjustments. Geotechnical analysis ensures reservoir stability, while data-driven decision-making refines water management and dispatch strategies.

Environmental Impact

By studying historical precipitation and water flow patterns, the project aims to optimize storage capacity and energy yield. This approach addresses the intermittency associated with renewable energy sources like solar and wind while improving resilience in remote communities.

Sustainable Development Goals

Aligned with the UN's SDG 7, the initiative seeks to guarantee access to affordable, reliable, and sustainable energy for all Indonesians. By enhancing energy independence, it fosters economic growth through improved energy access.
