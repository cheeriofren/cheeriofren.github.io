Exploring Natural Photosensitizer: Caesalpinia sappan in DSSC

Jan 2018 - Jan 2019 Associated with Kementerian Agama Republik Indonesia

The global energy crisis and fossil fuel dependency underscore the urgent need for sustainable energy solutions. This project, developed with the assistance of professors and students from UINAM Makassar, employs a natural dye from Caesalpinia sappan, recognized for its effective light absorption in dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSC).

Molecular Structure Visualization

Technical Implementation

Presented at the KSM Expo in 2019, the project won first place for developing a photovoltaic cell using secang wood dye as a photosensitizer and titanium dioxide (TiO₂) as the semiconductor anode. The dye enables photon adsorption, generating bound excitons that enhance electron excitation and mobility within the TiO₂ matrix, optimizing photoconversion efficiency.

Research Methodology

Characterization techniques such as UV-Vis spectroscopy, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, and current-voltage (I-V) measurements assessed optical properties and device performance. Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) analyzed TiO₂ nanostructures to ensure optimal surface area for dye adsorption.

Sustainable Impact

This initiative supports multiple Sustainable Development Goals, particularly Goal 7: Affordable and Clean Energy, advancing renewable technologies and improving energy efficiency. Winning first place at the KSM Expo 2019 emphasizes the project's significance in renewable energy and the importance of utilizing local resources for sustainability.
